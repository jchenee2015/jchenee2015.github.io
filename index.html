Dr. Jun Chen received his B.S. degree in Automation from Zhejiang University, Hangzhou China, in 2009, and Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering with minor in Computer Science from Iowa State University, Ames, IA, USA, in 2014. He was a research scientist with Idaho National Laboratory from 2014 to 2016. Since January 2017, Dr. Chen joined General Motors as a research engineer, working on advanced algorithms for vehicle motion and powertrain control.

His research interests include advanced control and optimization, model predictive control, machine learning, stochastic hybrid systems, with applications in energy and automotive systems. Dr. Chen is a recipient of the Best Transaction Paper Award from IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering, the Publication Achievement Award and the Exceptional Contributions Program Award (twice) from Idaho National Laboratory, the Research Excellence Award from Iowa State University, and the Outstanding Student Award from Zhejiang University. He has been a reviewer for numerous international scientific journals and conferences, and is/was an associate editor or editorial board member for Journal of Control and Decision, Energy Systems, and Chinese Control and Decision Conference.
