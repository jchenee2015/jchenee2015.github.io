    Jun Chen, Ph.D.

    Dr. Jun Chen received his B.S. degree in Automation from Zhejiang University, Hangzhou China, in 2009, and Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering with minor in Computer Science from Iowa State University, Ames, IA, USA, in 2014. He was a student intern for General Motors R&D in summer 2014. Dr. Chen joined Idaho National Laboratory, Idaho Falls, ID, USA, in November 2014, where he is currently a R&D Scientist.

    His research interests include discrete-event systems, stochastic hybrid systems, power and energy systems, together with their control, optimization, diagnosis, and resiliency analysis. Dr. Chen is a recipient of the Best Paper Award from IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering, the Publication Achievement Award and the Exceptional Contributions Program Award (twice) from Idaho National Laboratory, the Research Excellence Award from Iowa State University, and the Provost's Graduate Fellowship from University of Central Florida. He has been a reviewer for numerous scientific journals and conferences, and is an associate editor or editorial board member for Journal of Control and Decision, Energy Systems, and Chinese Control and Decision Conference. Currently, he is a member of the IEEE.
